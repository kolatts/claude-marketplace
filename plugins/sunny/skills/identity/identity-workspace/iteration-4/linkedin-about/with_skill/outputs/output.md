I'm a Staff Engineer at Verifiable, a healthcare credentialing SaaS. I lead source automation at the core of the product, run the Developer Experience guild, and mentor engineers across the org.

The path here wasn't straight. I started at Case Western Reserve University, Cleveland Clinic Lerner College of Medicine — completed most of my clinical rotations and walked away in my fourth year. Not because it wasn't going well. I just didn't like it, and the system made it harder to actually help people, not easier.

Between medicine and software I co-founded and recorded an album with Deadiron, a melodic thrash metal band. That taught me something that maps directly to engineering: a technically excellent team that operates like five separate people produces less than a locked-in one that moves like a single organism. No solos. No prima donnas. Just the work that serves the outcome.

The clinical training I left behind is genuinely useful now — when the domain is healthcare compliance and the end-users are the people the software is supposed to help, that context matters. The instinct for helping others never left.

Building an effective engineering org means more than writing good code or shipping on time. It means tooling that multiplies everyone's output, coaching engineers through hard problems, and building a culture that's collaborative and creative — while staying in touch with doing the actual work yourself. The best solutions solve five problems at once. The best outcomes are engineers who grow past what you could have done alone.

I also consult through Imagile, my AI-forward software practice.

I'm based in Columbus, Ohio.
