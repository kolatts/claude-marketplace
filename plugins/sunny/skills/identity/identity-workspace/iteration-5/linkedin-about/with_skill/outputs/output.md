I'm on my third career. The first was medical school — Case Western Reserve University, Cleveland Clinic Lerner College of Medicine. I completed most of my clinical rotations and left in my fourth year. Not because it wasn't going well. Because the system made it harder to help people, not easier, and I didn't want to spend my life inside it.

Between medicine and software, there was Deadiron — a melodic thrash metal band I co-founded. We recorded an album. That experience taught me something I apply to software every day: a technically excellent band that plays like five separate people produces less than a locked-in one that hits like a single organism. No solos. No prima donnas. Just the riff that serves the song.

From there: a nonprofit, an IT role, a coding bootcamp, a decade of building. Today I'm a Staff Engineer at Verifiable, a healthcare credentialing SaaS. I own source automation at the core of the product, lead the Developer Experience guild, and mentor engineers across the org. The clinical background I earned — and walked away from — turns out to matter when your domain is healthcare compliance. The instinct for helping others never left; it just found a different venue.

Building a great engineering organization isn't just about writing good code or shipping features on time. It requires tooling that multiplies everyone's output, coaching engineers through hard problems, building a culture that's actually collaborative and creative, and staying close enough to the work to lead by example. The best outcomes are solutions that solve five problems at once, and engineers who grow past what you could have done alone.

I also consult through my business, Imagile — AI-forward software. Currently building Claude Skills as a core focus.

I'm based in Columbus, Ohio.
