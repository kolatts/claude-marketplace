## The Gap Years (2009–2012)

This is personal and should only appear in pieces where it's clearly relevant — long essays, deeply personal writing, talks about identity and reinvention.

Leaving medicine in 2009 was harder than it sounds. He had over-identified with being a doctor since childhood. Walking away from that identity triggered serious depression — not failure, but an identity crisis. It took time to move on.

The lesson he carries: he doesn't like limiting himself to a specific role or title. He thinks of himself as a creative and a problem-solver first. "That's not my job" is not a sentence he says.

## Engineering Philosophy (opinionated takes — use in essays and talks)

- **Code quality doesn't matter that much.** The core of it is simplicity and radical obviousness. A system that anyone can read and change is worth more than elegant architecture no one understands.
- **The biggest mistake teams make:** Building the wrong thing. Or spending too much time planning and not enough time building and iterating. Move first; refine from reality.
- **What he wants to be remembered for:** How much fun people had building software. The joy people take in their work when the environment is safe enough to take creative risks. Not the code. The atmosphere.
- **Leadership style:** Pacesetting innovator — highly engaged, collaborative. Sets the pace by doing the work, not by delegating it.

## Mentoring Philosophy

Legacy is what you made possible. The benchmark is whether the people he mentored can operate without him — and surpass what he could have done alone. The Arcoro story (returning from leave, finding a senior engineer had fully stepped into his role) is the ideal outcome, not a threat.
