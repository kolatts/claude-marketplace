# Blog Craft Rules

## What makes Sunny's blog style distinctive

Posts reframe, not just assert. The analogy does the heavy lifting so the prose stays lean. A good analogy makes the reader feel like they already knew the answer — they just hadn't framed it that way.

- **Dry understatement as emphasis** — instead of "This is a significant advantage," write "This is kind of the point."
- **Own the observation** — "Every failed cloud migration I've seen..." is stronger than "Many organizations struggle with..." The first has credibility. The second has none.
- **First-person, opinionated** — own the perspective. Don't soften with "I think," "perhaps," or "many would argue."
- **State the uncomfortable thing plainly** — don't walk up to a hard truth and back away.
- **Short paragraphs** — three sentences is the ceiling. Most should be two. One-sentence paragraphs are for emphasis — use them when a line should land by itself.
- **Named concepts when they earn it** — if a well-known principle genuinely names what you're describing, cite it. Don't force one in to sound credible.

## Titles

Make a claim, not a topic. "Your Cloud Migration Failed Because You Skipped the Hard Part" beats "Cloud Migration Challenges."

Useful structures:
- `"Your X Is Already Y"` — when the audience is doing something wrong without knowing it
- `"X: The Y That Z"` — reframing a conventional choice as a strength
- `"Why Your X Failed Because You Y"` — diagnostic frame, implies the fix exists

Never: abstract nouns as titles. "The Future of AI in Development" says nothing.

## Section headers

State the point, not the subject. "It's Fast" beats "Performance Considerations." Headers are promises — curiosity beats explanation.

## What to always cut

- Any paragraph that begins with "At Imagile, we believe..."
- Bullet-list summaries at the end of sections — turn them into prose or cut them
- Phrases: "thought leadership," "cutting-edge," "best practices," "leverage" (as a verb), "robust"
- The word "journey" applied to anything technical
- Any CTA that ends with an exclamation point
- Hedges: "I think," "perhaps," "it seems like," "many would argue"
- Repeated restatements of the thesis ("As we've seen above...")
- ASCII diagrams
- Code blocks unless one short specific example genuinely earns its space
- Concept tables — if it could be prose, make it prose

## Rules

- Never open with "In today's fast-paced world" or any generic framing
- Max one analogy per post. Two is usually one too many
- Every analogy must make the argument clearer, not just colorful. If strained, drop it
- Mirror the rhythm: short declarative sentences, followed by a more developed thought, then move on
- Don't pad to hit a word count. A tight 600-word post beats a bloated 1,200-word one
